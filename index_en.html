About the Project

This project is based on the digitization of the archival passenger lists for Echelon ‚Ññ747. Until now, the data for these 2,403 passengers was not available in digital format and existed only as physical paper records.

We have converted these records into a digital registry and reconstructed the journey's timeline: the train departed from Saratov on September 7, 1941, and arrived at Oyash station (Novosibirsk region) ten days later, on September 17.

The route and dates were cross-referenced with the personal diaries of one of the passengers, Lyudmila Alexandrovna Gross. The project was created to make this information accessible for researchers and families tracing their history.

Methodology and Sources

Sources

The primary source consists of archival records for Echelon ‚Ññ747. We cross-referenced these records with personal testimonies from Lyudmila Alexandrovna Gross‚Äôs diaries to verify dates and geographical milestones.

Data Processing Steps

  • Transcription & Digitization: Converting archival records into a structured Excel/JSON format.
  • Normalization: Standardizing names to resolve inconsistencies in the original documents.
  • Toponymic Correction: Identifying and correcting historical place names.
  • Cleaning & Validation: Removing duplicates and verifying birth years.
  • Technical Transformation: Exporting data to JSON for instant search functionality.
  • Public Contribution: The digitized registry has been shared with the international "Memorial" project to supplement the database of political repression victims.
