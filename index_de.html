About the Project

This research is based on the digitization of archival passenger lists for Echelon ‚Ññ747. Until now, data for the 2,403 passengers of this train was unavailable in digital format and existed only as physical paper records in archives.

We have converted this data into a digital registry, processed it, and reconstructed the journey's timeline: on September 7, 1941, the echelon departed from Saratov and arrived at its destination‚ÄîOyash station (Novosibirsk region)‚Äîtwelve days later, on September 19.

The route and dates were cross-verified using the personal diaries of two passengers, Lyudmila Alexandrovna Gross and D. Bergman. The project aims to make this data accessible to historians and families searching for their relatives.

Methodology and Sources

Sources

The primary sources are the archival lists of Echelon ‚Ññ747 held at the State Archive of the Novosibirsk Region (GANO) and personal diaries of the participants: L. A. Gross and D. Bergman. The data has been verified and cross-referenced with geographical gazetteers of the Volga German ASSR.

Processing Stages

  • Transcription & Digitization: Converting primary archival records into a structured Excel format.
  • Normalization: Standardizing names to resolve inconsistencies in the original documents.
  • Toponymic Correction: Identifying and correcting historical names of settlements.
  • Cleaning & Validation: Removing duplicates, verifying birth years, and final structuring.
  • Translation: Translating the data into German and English.
  • Technical Transformation: Exporting to JSON for instant searching across 2,403 records.
  • Public Contribution: The registry has been shared with the international "Memorial" project to supplement the database of political repression victims.
